This guide will help you scale an object or image to **fill the entire canvas** (working area) of your project in GIMP.

---

## âš™ï¸ Prerequisites (Layer Selection)

Before you begin, the element you want to scale must be on a **separate layer**.

1.  Ensure the desired layer is active by clicking on it in the **Layers Panel** (usually located on the right).
2.  If your element is in an alpha channel, make sure to **convert the alpha channel to a selection** before using the scale tool.

---

## Step 1: Access the Scale Tool

Once the layer is selected, start the transformation tool:

* **Main Menu:**
    > Tools > Transform Tools > **Scale**
* **Keyboard Shortcut:**
    > **Shift + T**

When activated, a box with adjustable handles (small squares) will appear around the selected object.

---

## Step ðŸ“ Step 2: Define and Adjust the Size

The object will be selected with a box that has adjustable vertices and edges.

1.  **Maintain Proportions:** In the Scale tool dialog box, make sure the **chain icon** between the **Width** and **Height** fields is **closed (linked)**. This is crucial to prevent the image from being distorted.
2.  **Verify Canvas:** Check your canvas dimensions (Main Menu > Image > Canvas Size...).
3.  **Adjust Measurements:** Enter the desired measurements for the layer's **Width** and **Height**, matching them with your canvas dimensions.

> ðŸ’¡ **Tip for Proportions:** If you want to maintain the image's original proportion, only enter the larger measurement (Width or Height). GIMP will automatically calculate the other value if the chain icon is closed.

---

## Step 3: Apply the Transformation

1.  Once the dimensions are correct and match your canvas, click the **Scale** button in the dialog box.

Your element will have been enlarged and will fill the entire canvas space, completing the "page" of your project.
